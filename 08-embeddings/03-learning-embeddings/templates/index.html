Learning Embeddings

Train Word2Vec from scratch — watch random vectors organize by meaning

The Training Task: Predict Context

We don't tell the network what words mean. We give it one simple task: given a center word, predict the words around it.

In the sentence "the cat sat on the mat", with center word "cat" and window size 2:

Training pairs from "the cat sat on":
cat → predict the  |  cat → predict sat

Architecture: center_word → embedding matrix (V × 2) → predict context
The embedding matrix IS the result. Each row = one word's vector.
Words that predict similar contexts get similar embeddings.

"Cat" and "dog" both appear near "the", "sat", "on", "ran" — so backprop pushes their embeddings closer together. No one told the network they're both animals.

Things to Try

  • Click "Train Word2Vec" then step through the epoch checkpoints. At epoch 0, words are scattered randomly. By epoch 50-100, animals cluster together, verbs cluster together, adjectives cluster together.
  • Look at the nearest neighbors. After training, "cat"'s neighbors are "dog" and "bird". "big"'s neighbors are "small". Nobody programmed this — it emerged from context prediction.
  • Watch the loss curve. Loss drops rapidly in the first 20 epochs as the model learns the most common patterns, then slowly refines.
  • Increase epochs to 300. More training = tighter clusters. But diminishing returns — most structure appears by epoch 50.
  • The corpus is tiny (~100 words). Real Word2Vec trains on billions of words. With more data, the embeddings capture much finer distinctions — verb tenses, singular/plural, analogies like king-man+woman=queen.
