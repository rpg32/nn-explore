From One Query to All Queries

Module 09.2 showed attention for one query word. Now we compute it for every word simultaneously. Each word asks: "which other words are relevant to me?" This produces an N×N matrix.

Q = X × W_Q    (N words × d_k — every word gets a query)
K = X × W_K    (N words × d_k — every word gets a key)
scores = Q × KT / √d_k    (N × N matrix — every query vs every key)
weights = softmax(scores)    (each row sums to 1)
That's just three matrix multiplies. All words processed in parallel. No loop.

Who Attends to Whom?

Reading the Matrix

Row i = "when processing word i, how much does it attend to each other word?"

Each row sums to 1 — it's a probability distribution.

Bright cells = high attention. Word i strongly attends to word j.

Diagonal = a word attending to itself (often high — the word itself is relevant to its own representation).

In Module 09.4 (Self-Attention), we'll see this matrix used inside the transformer itself — not between encoder and decoder, but within a single sequence attending to itself.

Things to Try

  • "the cat sat on the mat" — look at the row for "cat". It should attend most to "dog"-like or noun-like words if present. The two "the" tokens may attend to different things.
  • "the big dog chased the small cat" — does "big" attend to "dog"? Does "small" attend to "cat"? Adjectives should attend to what they modify.
  • "a quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog" — a longer sentence. More patterns visible in the matrix.
  • Compare raw scores vs weights. Raw scores can be any number. Softmax squashes them so each row sums to 1 and the strongest connection dominates.
  • Notice the structure. The matrix isn't random — semantic patterns emerge even with untrained projection matrices, because the embeddings already encode category similarity.
