Module 02 · How Learning Works

A Neuron Learns

Everything comes together — watch a neuron train itself

This is the payoff. We combine every concept from Modules 01 and 02 into the training loop — the heart of all machine learning:

1. FORWARD PASS — feed data through, get predictions
2. COMPUTE LOSS — how wrong are the predictions? (BCE)
3. COMPUTE GRADIENTS — which way should each weight move?
4. UPDATE WEIGHTS — nudge weights downhill
5. REPEAT — until the loss is small and accuracy is high

Load a dataset, then step through one training iteration at a time. Watch the decision boundary move as the neuron learns.

Things to Try

  • Load "Simple", click Step x1 repeatedly. Watch the decision boundary swing into place one step at a time. This is learning happening before your eyes.
  • Run x50 and see the loss plummet and accuracy jump to 100%. The neuron found the right weights on its own!
  • Load "Diagonal". The boundary needs to rotate. Watch how w1 and w2 change sign as it swings.
  • Load "Messy". The clusters overlap. The neuron will find the BEST line, but accuracy will be less than 100%. Some data just can't be perfectly separated by a line.
  • Reset and try lr = 0.1 vs lr = 10. How does the learning speed change? Does high lr cause oscillation?
  • Watch the weights in the info panel as you step. The gradients are pushing them toward the right values. This is the algorithm deciding on its own where to draw the line.
