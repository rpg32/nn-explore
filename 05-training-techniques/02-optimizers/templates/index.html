Optimizers

Why plain gradient descent is slow, and how Momentum, RMSProp, and Adam fix it

SGD: w -= lr × gradient
Momentum: v = 0.9×v + grad;   w -= lr × v
RMSProp: s = 0.999×s + 0.001×grad²;   w -= lr × grad / √s
Adam: momentum + RMSProp + bias correction   (the default)

Things to Try

  • Elongated Bowl — SGD oscillates wildly across the steep axis. Momentum smooths it out. Adam and RMSProp head straight to the minimum.
  • Rosenbrock Valley — the curved narrow valley. SGD gets stuck. Momentum overshoots around the bend. Adam navigates the curve.
  • Crank the LR up on the landscape. SGD goes unstable first. Adam stays robust longest.
  • Neural network comparison. With 50 epochs, all optimizers reach similar accuracy — but look at the loss curve. Adam and Momentum converge in ~10 epochs. SGD takes 30+.
  • Cut epochs to 10. Now the advantage of Adam is clear — it converges fast where SGD barely starts.
