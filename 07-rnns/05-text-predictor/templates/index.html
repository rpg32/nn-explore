Build a Text Predictor

Train a character-level LSTM and watch it discover English — one letter at a time

🔑 Key Insight

The model has NEVER been told English grammar or spelling. It discovers patterns purely from seeing characters one at a time and trying to predict the next one. After enough training, it learns that 'q' is followed by 'u', that spaces come after words, and that "th" usually leads to "e". This is the foundation of all modern language models.

Things to Try

  • Train 10 epochs and generate with seed "the ". It will be mostly gibberish. Train 50 more and try again — you'll see real words emerge.
  • Compare temperatures: Generate from "the " at 0.2 (safe, repetitive) vs 1.5 (chaotic). Low temperature picks the most likely character every time. High temperature rolls the dice.
  • Use the Prediction View with "the cat sat on" before and after training. Before: random guesses. After: it predicts ' ' after words and 'e' after "th".
  • Try a seed the model hasn't seen like "a fish" — it still generates plausible text because it learned character-level patterns, not memorized sentences.
  • Reset and retrain at different learning rates. 0.001 is slow but stable. 0.05 learns fast but might oscillate. Watch the loss curve shape change.
  • Train 200+ epochs — the model will start to memorize the training text. This is overfitting: great at reproducing training data, but less creative on new seeds.
